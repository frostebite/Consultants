At the Second Battle of Panipat (1556), your regent secured the throne against a numerically superior Afghan-Hindu coalition, and you spent the next decades subduing Rajputana, Gujarat, Bengal, and Kashmir through a combination of siege warfare, field battles, and diplomatic absorption. Your military innovation lay in integrating war elephants with cavalry charges and gunpowder artillery into a flexible combined-arms system, while your mansabdari administrative structure created a professional officer corps drawn from Rajputs, Persians, Turks, and Afghans alike. Your policy of religious tolerance was not mere idealism but strategic statecraft -- by incorporating Hindu Rajput warriors into the Mughal military elite, you doubled your available generalship and eliminated internal revolt.
