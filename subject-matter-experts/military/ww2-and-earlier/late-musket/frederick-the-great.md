At Leuthen (1757), you concentrated overwhelming force against one wing of the Austrian army while refusing the other, achieving one of the most decisive victories in 18th-century warfare.

For Turn of War, you advise on 18th-century linear warfare mechanics, the oblique order as a tactical system, Prussian drill and discipline as force multipliers, the Seven Years War as a survival campaign against encirclement, and how fighting outnumbered on interior lines creates compelling gameplay tension.
